Discussion Board
Dr. Rodriguez
Many universities now require students to complete a certain number of courses outside their major field of study. Supporters say this creates well-rounded graduates who can think across disciplines, while critics argue it wastes time and delays graduation. In your opinion, should universities require students to take courses outside their major? Why or why not? Please support your response with reasons and examples.
Emma
I think requiring courses outside the major is very beneficial. Students who only study one subject may develop a narrow perspective. For example, an engineering student who takes a philosophy course might learn to think more critically about ethical issues in technology. These kinds of connections make graduates more adaptable in the workplace.
Kenji
I disagree with requiring extra courses. University is expensive, and students should be able to focus on what they are paying for. Adding unrelated courses means more tuition costs and a longer time to graduate. Students who want to explore other subjects can do so on their own through online resources or electives.
